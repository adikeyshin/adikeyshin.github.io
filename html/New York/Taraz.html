TARAZ

Taraz city, administrative center of Zhambyl region of Kazakhstan. Located in the south of the country, near the border with Kyrgyzstan, on the Talas River. The population of the city is 428,000 people (as of April 2023)[3]. There is an international airport, a railway station, bus stations, shopping centers, entertainment complexes, and many public gardens and parks. Higher educational institutions: Dzhambul Irrigation and Construction Institute (with a branch in the city of Karatau), Dzhambul Institute of Light and Food Industry and Dzhambul Pedagogical Institute. In 1998, Zhambyl Irrigation and Construction, Zhambyl Institute of Light and Food Industry and Zhambyl University were merged into Taraz State University named after M. Kh. Dulati. There is also a private Taraz Innovation and Humanities University (TIGU), branches of the university named after. Khoja Akhmet Yassawi, Almaty Institute of Economics and Statistics.
